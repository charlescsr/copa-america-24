Day 2

Peru v Chile

Boring. Wild shooting. The battle of the old pros still flying the flags end with a point. This was a match with so many old pros who were STILL playing for both these countries. And in the end, none of them could finish their chances because we only got a grand total of 5 shots on target. 4 from Peru and 1 from Chile which says to how stupid this game really was.

Match Reaction

I’m resorting to this whenever I see a dud 0-0 from here on in.

We start with a long goal kick which found the winger on the right and his cross was cushioned but the Peru player couldn’t take advantage of a mess up in the defense and the shot was horribly weak towards a 41 year old Claudio Bravo which is a crazy statement to say. The next one was a good cross from the center which was kept in and Lapadula who actually hit the weak shot from last time controlled it well through a ton of Chile bodies but his shot blazed over the bar. To make it even worse it was called out for a foul against the player. Chile got an opportunity when Peru were caught out playing from the back but Nunez(Not Darwin don’t worry) took a pretty tame shot towards the goal but given he was under pressure I can understand it. There was another chance from Lapadula when he was left unmarked from a Peru corner but his shot wasn’t timed right and Bravo was able to save it and hold onto it. There was a final chance from Brereton Diaz when a ball came in perfectly for him but he blazed over and that was kind of the theme of this match entirely. Too many off target shots.

Player of the Match - A. Sanchez

I know for a fact he can always turn up for his national team despite what he does at club level. He was decent for Inter Milan in their Scudetto winning season. Had a okay game and was creating good chances but missed some as well. Best in a bad bunch I guess.
