Day 1

Argentina v Canada

The definition of toying with the opposition. Martinez brings his shooting boots from Milan. Canada goalie the lone bright spark in an underwhelming showing. An absolutely comfortable win for the holders Argentina as they were relatively comfortable and took their goals well. Let’s get into the reaction.

1st Half

This half was good from Argentina. We got a chance as early as minute 5 with a shot from Acuna being blocked and Paredes blazing a shot over. Di Maria had a solid chance but his chance was saved well by the keeper who was undoubtedly at his best as he made a lot of solid saves. We had one great chance from Messi at the end of the half but his shot was too high. There was also a solid save from Dibu Martinez to still keep the score level. It was one of those saves where even if there didn’t seem to be much threat but when called upon he delivered.

2nd Half

The half started well from Argentina’s perspective as Messi played a beautiful ball to MacAllister who nudged it towards Alvarez, took a tumble and Alvarez made no mistake with the finish. I appreciated him actually checking in on MacAllister because he was still down after he scored. Alvarez had another go from the centre but it was saved by the keeper. Messi had another golden chance after being sent through from Dibu Martinez but in the end the other keeper stood firm to deny him. The final nail eventually came when Lautaro Martinez who was subbed on for Alvarez managed to finish it well after another perfect through ball from Messi.
